FIBROIDS AND BLACK WOMEN — HEALTH EDUCATION GUIDE

What Are Fibroids?
Uterine fibroids are noncancerous growths that develop in or on the uterus. They are also called leiomyomas or myomas. Fibroids range in size from tiny seedlings to large masses that can distort the shape of the uterus. A woman can have one fibroid or many.

Why Black Women Are Disproportionately Affected
Black women are 2 to 3 times more likely to develop fibroids than white women. They also tend to develop fibroids at a younger age, have more fibroids, and experience more severe symptoms. Despite this higher risk, Black women are less likely to receive timely diagnosis and treatment due to barriers in healthcare access and a history of being dismissed by medical providers.

Symptoms
Many women with fibroids have no symptoms. When symptoms do occur they may include:
- Heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding
- Pelvic pain or pressure
- Frequent urination
- Difficulty emptying the bladder
- Constipation
- Back or leg pain
- Pain during sex

Severe heavy bleeding can lead to anemia, causing fatigue and weakness.

Causes and Risk Factors
The exact cause of fibroids is unknown. Factors that may increase risk include:
- Race — Black women are at significantly higher risk
- Family history — fibroids tend to run in families
- Hormones — estrogen and progesterone promote fibroid growth
- Age — fibroids are most common during reproductive years
- Obesity
- Early onset of menstruation

Treatment Options
Treatment depends on symptoms, fibroid size, location, and whether you plan to have children. Options include:

Watchful Waiting — If fibroids are small and causing no symptoms, monitoring without treatment may be appropriate.

Medications — Hormonal medications can help manage symptoms but do not eliminate fibroids. Options include birth control pills, GnRH agonists, and progestin-releasing IUDs.

Minimally Invasive Procedures:
- Uterine Fibroid Embolization (UFE) — blocks blood supply to fibroids causing them to shrink
- Radiofrequency Ablation — destroys fibroid tissue using heat
- MRI-guided focused ultrasound — uses sound waves to destroy fibroids

Surgical Options:
- Myomectomy — removes fibroids while preserving the uterus, recommended for women who want to become pregnant
- Hysterectomy — complete removal of the uterus, the only permanent cure for fibroids

Advocating For Yourself
Black women are more likely to have their pain dismissed or symptoms minimized by healthcare providers. If you feel your concerns are not being heard:
- Keep a symptom journal to share with your doctor
- Ask specifically about fibroid testing if you have symptoms
- Seek a second opinion if needed
- Request a referral to a specialist such as a gynecologist or urogynecologist
